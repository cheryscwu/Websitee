Design Area 2.1 — Alternative Water Harvesting & Treatment

Addressing freshwater shortages through innovative water collection and purification systems

The Water Crisis on Saibai Island

Saibai Island is a small island located in the Torres Strait near the northern tip of Australia, which faces severe freshwater shortages throughout the year. The island is extremely low-lying, with most areas less than one meter above sea level, and it has no rivers or natural freshwater lakes.

Saibai Island - Maximum Elevation: 1.7m above sea level

As a result, the local community relies almost entirely on an artificial storage lagoon for its water supply. However, rainfall in the region is highly seasonal, and during the long dry season, stored water often runs out. Most of the island's groundwater has high turbidity (according to the EWB website, the unprocessed underground water on Saibai Island is above 5 NTU) and therefore unsuitable for drinking.

Key Challenges

Water Shortages

During the long dry season, stored water often runs out, forcing reliance on external water transportation.

Water Quality

High turbidity in groundwater makes it unsuitable for drinking without proper treatment.

Climate Threats

Rising sea levels, flooding, and saltwater intrusion further threaten existing freshwater resources.

Infrastructure Needs

Saibai Island is one of the Australian communities under the greatest freshwater stress, highlighting the urgent need for improved infrastructure.

Why Water Harvesting & Treatment Matters

On Saibai Island, rainwater harvesting and water treatment are extremely important because rainfall is highly seasonal, and long dry periods often lead to water shortages. Therefore, efficient water collection systems can provide residents with sufficient water throughout the year to meet their daily needs.

At the same time, water treatment is essential to ensure that the stored water is safe for drinking and household use. Without proper treatment, collected water may contain harmful bacteria or impurities. Improving both water harvesting and treatment systems is crucial for Saibai Island's long-term water security, public health, and its ability to cope with climate change and rising sea levels.

Design Requirements

Extreme Weather Resilience

First and most importantly, Saibai Island is frequently affected by extreme weather conditions. Therefore, this system must be designed to be highly reliable, easy to maintain, and capable of operating independently. The purpose of such a design is to ensure that even during extreme weather events, the system can continue to function properly to meet the residents' water needs.

Water Quality Standards

According to the brief on EWB, the Water Filtration System must need to be designed to meet the standard that reducing the turbidity in water to below 1NTU which is classified as high water quality.
