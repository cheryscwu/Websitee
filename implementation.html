Overview

The implementation of the dual water system is designed to promote sustainable construction, community participation, and long-term management. Collaboration between the Torres Strait Island Regional Council (TSIRC), Engineers Without Borders (EWB), and Saibai Island residents will ensure the system is both technically sound and community-driven.

Project Timeline

Months 1-4

Construction and Community Training

The non-potable water system will involve installing leaf guards and gutters on rooftops to collect rainwater into sealed storage tanks. Using locally sourced materials will simplify future maintenance and reduce replacement costs.

TSIRC technicians and EWB volunteers will facilitate hands-on training sessions to equip residents with practical skills in cleaning leaf guards, inspecting pipe connections, and managing storage tanks.

For the potable water system, lagoon water will pass through a settling tank, multi-layer filtration, an ultrafiltration membrane, and a UV disinfection unit. Community members will receive training on maintaining filters, replacing membranes, and operating the UV disinfection system safely.

Months 5-10

System Introduction, Management, and Evaluation

The system will be introduced through workshops and demonstration sessions that allow residents to understand and operate the technology. Illustrated instruction manuals in the local language will support daily use and maintenance.

Regular maintenance, such as monthly filter cleaning and annual inspections, will be coordinated by the TSIRC environmental health team. Routine turbidity testing will ensure water quality remains below 1 NTU, while community feedback and annual sustainability reviews will evaluate system effectiveness and satisfaction.

Key Milestone 1

Illustrated instruction manuals distributed

Key Milestone 2

Regular maintenance schedule established

Key Milestone 3

First water quality assessment completed

Months 11-18

Community Ownership, Future Impact, and Repair

Over time, operational responsibility will be transferred to trained community teams, fostering independence and long-term ownership. The project will enhance water security, public health, and local technical capacity.

Minor faults will be repaired by community technicians using available toolkits, while TSIRC will assist with more complex technical issues.

Stakeholder Roles & Responsibilities

Torres Strait Island Regional Council (TSIRC)

Coordinates implementation, provides technical oversight, assists with complex technical issues, and leads the environmental health team for regular maintenance.

Engineers Without Borders (EWB)

Provides technical expertise, facilitates training sessions, supports system design refinement, and assists with capacity building.

Saibai Island Residents

Active participation in construction and training, daily operation and maintenance of systems, providing feedback, and gradually taking ownership of the water systems.

Community Technicians

Trained residents responsible for minor repairs using available toolkits, routine maintenance tasks, and serving as local points of contact for system issues.

Monitoring & Evaluation Framework

Water Quality Monitoring

Regular turbidity testing to ensure water quality remains below 1 NTU target. Additional testing for biological contaminants as needed.

System Performance

Tracking system uptime, maintenance needs, and component lifespan to assess reliability and identify improvement areas.

Community Satisfaction

Regular feedback collection through surveys and community meetings to gauge satisfaction and identify concerns.

Capacity Building

Assessment of local technical skills development and community ownership of the water systems.

Expected Impact: The project will enhance water security, public health, and local technical capacity. Minor faults will be repaired by community technicians using available toolkits, while TSIRC will assist with more complex technical issues.
