Problem Statement

Saibai Island's community requires a dependable and sustainable water system that maintains turbidity below 1 NTU to ensure high-quality and safe water for consumption, meeting proper standards and improving overall health and wellbeing.

Current Water Challenges

Saibai Island experiences ongoing challenges in maintaining both the quality and quantity of its water supply. The main water sources, such as rainwater tanks and lagoon water catchments, often have turbidity levels ranging from 1 to 2 NTU, which exceed the acceptable limitable set by Torres Strait Island Regional Council (TSRIC) for safe drinking water.

Community Impacts:

  • Residents frequently rely on costly transported water and diesel-powered desalination units to meet their daily needs
  • Public health risks due to inadequate water quality
  • Financial and environmental pressures on the community
  • Lack of confidence in existing water systems
Primary Objective: The main goal is to implement a simple, low-cost, and energy-efficient water system that consistently maintains turbidity below 1 NTU, providing clean and safe drinking water that can be effectively managed by local residents.

Design Criteria

The following design criteria were developed to evaluate and guide the selection of the most suitable water management solution for Saibai Island. This criterion was chosen based on Torres Strait Island Regional Council's (TSIRC) priorities and the community's specific needs, ensuring the final design remains practical, sustainable, and culturally appropriate.

1Water Quality (Turbidity and Safety)

The design must guarantee that treated water consistently satisfies the turbidity criterion set by the Torres Strait Island Regional Council (TSRIC) of less than 1 NTU, providing safe and clean water for daily consumption. Consistently achieving this level will limit contamination and safeguard the community from diseases caused by unsafe water.

Ensuring low turbidity directly improves water safety and public health outcomes. Meeting TSIRC's standard reflects the commitment to delivering sustainable and high-quality water for Saibai Island residents (EWB Challenge, 2025).

2Economic Feasibility and Operational Simplicity

The system should remain affordable to build, operate, and maintain by using durable materials and straightforward technologies available locally. Due to Saibai Island's isolation and limited technical capacity, keeping costs low and maintenance simple is essential for long-term operation.

Affordable and user-friendly designs allow residents to maintain the system independently, ensuring long-term sustainability. This approach supports TSIRC's mission to provide an "adequate, safe and reliable water supply" to all island communities (Torres Strait Island Regional Council, n.d).

3Reliability and Resilience

The system must consistently supply safe water under various weather conditions, including heavy rainfall, flooding, and periods of limited rainfall. It should include storage and protective features that adapt to Saibai's tropical climate.

A resilient and reliable system safeguards community health and ensures continuous water access throughout the year. This criterion addresses the island's environmental challenges while supporting long-term water security goals (EWB Challenge, 2025).

Design Philosophy: These criteria ensure that the selected water management solution will not only meet technical requirements but also align with community capabilities, environmental conditions, and long-term sustainability goals.
