Design Solution Option — Solution 2

Home › Design › Solution 2

Overview

For this solution, water is collected from the lagoon and passed through the coarse screening[1]. This can remove floating debris and litter from the water flow. Then the waterflow is distributed to each household through the tube system. The multi-stage filter devices are added at the inlet of each household, and water is disinfected with chlorine.

The advantage is that this approach reduces additional centralized treatment infrastructure. However, residents need to maintain the filtration device themselves and monitor chlorine, which increases the O&M burden.

Uses existing tube network Lower CapEx centrally Household O&M required Chlorine checks

Process (Lagoon → Household)

  1. 1
    Lagoon intake
    Raw water drawn from lagoon
    ▶

    Abstraction from the lagoon under suitable tide/turbidity windows. Intake flow is conveyed to the coarse screen bay.

  2. 2
    Coarse screening
    Remove floating debris & litter [1]
    ▶

    Bar screens / trash racks intercept large solids and plastics to protect downstream pipes and household filters.

    Protects network Simple O&M
  3. 3
    Distribution
    Piped to households via existing tube system
    ▶

    Water is conveyed through the community pipework to each dwelling, minimizing new centralized facilities.

    Leverages existing assets Lower central CapEx
  4. 4
    Household multi-stage filtration
    5 μm → 1 μm → activated carbon (at inlet)
    ▶

    Each house installs a compact train (5 μm → 1 μm → carbon) to polish turbidity and taste/odor right before use.

    Point-of-entry control Improved taste
  5. 5
    Chlorine disinfection
    Dose & verify residual
    ▶

    Free chlorine provides a final barrier and residual in the small network. Residents perform simple residual checks (DPD kit).

    Final barrier Residual protection
  6. 6
    Point of use
    Household taps
    ▶

    Water is delivered at taps within each house. User SOPs cover filter change intervals and residual monitoring.

    Simple SOPs Household ownership
