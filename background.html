Location & Environmental Context

Saibai Island is situated in the low-lying north-west region of the Torres Strait. Due to its coastal position, the island faces increasing vulnerability to climate change effects such as saltwater intrusion and tidal inundation, both of which seriously threaten freshwater availability.

The primary water sources include the central lagoon and household rainwater harvesting systems. However, the high turbidity and potential contamination of these sources indicate pressing water quality challenges.

Community & Infrastructure Challenges

Saibai’s remote location makes logistics difficult, increasing costs and complexity for maintenance and infrastructure delivery. These limitations affect both the local community and the Torres Strait Island Regional Council (TSIRC), which oversees water management operations.

Public Health Impact

Low-quality water increases the likelihood of waterborne diseases, directly affecting community health and wellbeing.

Energy Use

Residents rely on boiling water for disinfection — a safe but energy-intensive method that raises household expenses.

Trust & Reliability

Inconsistent supply quality reduces public confidence in TSIRC’s water systems and management strategies.

The new regulatory standard requires potable water turbidity to remain below 1 NTU. Yet the current system achieves only 1–2 NTU, failing to meet this threshold, endangering public health and eroding trust in local water governance.

Technical Assessment

The current media filtration system, using five-micron and one-micron filters, cannot adequately reduce turbidity. Its output remains between 1–2 NTU, showing the need for upgraded filtration technology capable of achieving consistent clarity below the standard.

Although lagoon water management exists, efficiency remains low. Water treatment is uniformly applied, leading to resource wastage for non-potable uses. Chlorine disinfection adds further risk, as inconsistent dosing between lagoon and household tanks makes maintaining a uniform standard difficult.

Design Objective

The target design aims to create an efficient, practical, and low-cost system that provides potable water with turbidity below 1 NTU. A dual-source setup will separate potable and non-potable supply, improving efficiency, reducing waste, and ensuring sustainable management by the community.
