Design Solution Option — Solution 1

Home › Design › Solution 1

Overview

Each household directly collect the rainwater from the rooftop and store in the underground water tank. Before water storage in water tank, they just through a simple leaf guard. Then the waterflow passes through the multi-stage filtration, including 5μm, 1μm and active carbon filters [1]. Using solar-power UV light disinfect water. The advantage of this approach is that the demand of tube network is short. However, this approach relies on rainfall. In addition, it requires high standards for roof materials. This situation will increase the cost of initial building costs.

Rainwater harvesting Household-scale Short pipe network Solar-powered UV

Process (Household System)

  1. 1
    Roof capture
    Rainfall captured on roof → gutter with leaf guard
    ▶

    Each household collects rainwater directly from the rooftop. A simple leaf guard prevents large debris from entering the system before storage.

    Debris control Low-cost pre-screen
  2. 2
    Storage
    Conveyed to underground tank (non-pressurised)
    ▶

    Water flows into the underground water tank, reducing temperature swings and algae risk compared with above-ground tanks.

    Stable temperature Lower algae risk
  3. 3
    Multi-stage filtration
    5 μm → 1 μm → activated carbon
    ▶

    Water passes sequentially through 5 μm and 1 μm cartridges and then activated carbon to improve taste/odor and adsorb organics [1].

    Particle removal Taste & odor
  4. 4
    Disinfection
    Solar-powered UV final barrier
    ▶

    A compact UV unit powered by solar completes disinfection before use at the tap, delivering the final microbial barrier.

    Low energy Final barrier
  5. 5
    Point of use
    Short pipe runs to taps; reduced losses
    ▶

    Water is delivered at the point-of-use. Short tube network lowers headloss and simplifies maintenance. Reliance on rainfall and roof material standards remain key constraints.

    Short pipe length Easy O&M Rain-dependent
