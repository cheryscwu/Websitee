Design Selection – Solution A

Home › Design › Selection A

Overview

Solution A implements a decentralized rainwater harvesting system with multi-stage filtration and UV disinfection. While effective for water purification, this solution faces significant challenges related to climate dependency, soil conditions, and scalability on Saibai Island.

Key Challenge

This solution relies heavily on rainwater availability and faces implementation difficulties due to Saibai Island's unique geographical and climatic conditions.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • High water safety rating (4.0/5) due to multi-stage filtration
  • Solar-powered UV disinfection reduces energy dependency
  • Short pipe network minimizes maintenance requirements
  • Underground storage reduces temperature fluctuations

Limitations

  • Heavy reliance on rainfall, especially problematic during dry season (Dec-Apr)
  • High installation costs due to underground tank requirements in soft soil
  • Soil conditions (soft alluvial mud) complicate implementation
  • Poor scalability for dispersed household distribution
  • Requires high-standard roof materials, increasing costs

Geographical Constraints

Saibai Island Topography - Maximum elevation: 1.7m above sea level

Saibai Island's low-lying alluvial mud composition (maximum elevation only 1.7m above sea level) presents significant challenges for underground tank installation. The soft soil requires specialized technical support, increasing both initial construction and long-term maintenance costs.

Implementation Considerations

The dispersed distribution of small households across Saibai Island makes this solution particularly challenging to implement at scale. While effective for individual households in theory, the geographical and climatic constraints significantly limit its practical application across the entire community.
