Detailed Design

Home › Design › Detailed Design

Our design approach is based on differentiated treatments for rainwater and lagoon water, providing appropriate purification for each source according to its intended use.

Overview

Our entire design approach is based on differentiated treatments, providing appropriate water purification systems for different water sources and uses on Saibai Island. This ensures efficient resource use while meeting the community's diverse water needs.

Key Benefits

Cost-Effective Sustainable Community-Owned Easy Maintenance

Rainwater Harvesting System

1Initial Filtration

We implemented a unique system to prevent debris from flowing down the eaves along with the rainwater. This system's main component is a leaf guard that allows rainwater to flow down while trapping debris like leaves on the eaves' parapet.

2Storage and Usage

After initial filtration, the water is sent to a dedicated water tank for immediate use in tasks like plant fertilization and cleaning. The polyethylene filter provides long-term stability and can be easily cleaned weekly by island residents.

Lagoon Water Purification System

For lagoon water which must satisfy direct drinking standards, we employ a sophisticated multi-stage purification procedure.

1Circular Sedimentation Tank

The process begins in a cylindrical sedimentation tank with a conical bottom. Water enters from the side, creating swirling currents that use centrifugal force to separate heavy particles like silt.

2Mixed Filtration System

Water then passes through three consecutive standard ten-inch filter cartridges:

  • First cartridge: 5-micron PP fiber filter that collects remaining suspended solids
  • Second cartridge: Compressed activated alumina that absorbs organic compounds, chlorine, and odors
  • Third cartridge: Granular activated alumina for further purification

3Ultra Filtration Membrane Assembly

This third purification barrier uses hollow fiber ultra filter membranes with 0.01 micron pores that are impervious to viruses, bacteria, and colloids, maintaining effluent turbidity consistently below 1 NTU. The system includes a built-in backwashing feature that periodically removes impurities from the membrane surface.

4UV Sterilization

The final stage occurs in a sealed UV sterilization chamber where short-wave ultraviolet light disinfects the water by disrupting microbial DNA. This physical cleaning process requires no chemicals, eliminating the risk of further contamination.

Materials and System Design

Durable Piping

All water supply tubes are made from UPVC, which is long-lasting and corrosion-resistant.

Bypass System

All major treatment units include bypass piping, allowing filter media changes or equipment maintenance without stopping the entire system.

Easy Maintenance

The system is designed with comprehensive maintenance procedures that can be performed by local operators.

Maintenance Procedures

Daily

  • Leak checks

Weekly

  • Filter purification
  • Leaf guard cleaning

Monthly

  • Sludge elimination from sedimentation tank

Every 3–6 Months

  • Filter replacement at each level
  • UV lamp checks

Annually

  • Chemical cleaning of ultrafiltration membranes

Monitoring and Operation

To ensure effective monitoring, we recommend enhanced standards for operators, including regular reporting of final water quality standards. This allows us to monitor the effectiveness of the purification process and use data to demonstrate the system's reliability.

The entire design is practical for widespread implementation on Saibai Island, with special filter parts and unit membranes readily available for replacement when needed.
