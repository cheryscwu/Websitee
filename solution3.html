Design Solution Option — Solution 3

Home › Design › Solution 3

Overview

This solution divides the water resource into drinkable and undrinkable streams. Undrinkable water comes from rooftops and is used for flushing and watering plants. Drinkable water comes from the lagoon and passes through a swirl settling tank[1], then through multi-stage filtration and UV disinfection. Finally, clean water is stored in the existing main storage tank and distributed to households via the underground tube system.

This approach maximizes the use of water resources. The swirl settling tank primarily relies on gravity to remove mud and debris, reducing energy demand and simplifying maintenance.

Dual-stream (potable / non-potable) Gravity-based swirl settling Re-use existing storage & network Maximise resource use

Process (Split Resource → Potable Pathway)

  1. 1
    Non-potable stream
    Rooftop rainwater → flushing / garden
    ▶

    Rainwater captured from roofs is routed for non-potable uses such as toilet flushing and irrigation, reducing demand on potable supply.

    Reduce potable demand Simple use cases
  2. 2
    Lagoon intake
    Raw water for potable treatment
    ▶

    Water is abstracted from the lagoon during suitable windows and conveyed to the swirl settling tank for grit/mud removal.

  3. 3
    Swirl settling tank
    Gravity-driven removal of mud & debris [1]
    ▶

    Induces a controlled swirl to keep solids away from the outlet and settle rapidly to the bottom, decreasing load on downstream filters.

    Low energy Lower filter load
  4. 4
    Multi-stage filtration
    5 μm → 1 μm → activated carbon
    ▶

    Cartridge filtration removes fine particles and activated carbon polishes taste/odor before disinfection.

    Turbidity control Taste & odor
  5. 5
    UV disinfection
    Final barrier before storage
    ▶

    UV provides pathogen inactivation without chemicals and prepares water for storage and distribution.

    No chemical residual Microbial barrier
  6. 6
    Existing main storage tank
    Buffer capacity, level control
    ▶

    Treated water is stored in the current main tank, leveraging existing assets and providing buffer during demand peaks.

    Reuse assets Operational buffer
  7. 7
    Distribution to households
    Underground tube system
    ▶

    Water flows through the underground pipe network to each home for end use.

    Existing network Scalable
