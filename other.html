Holistic Design Approach

Not only consider these, but also consider culture, environmental and community. The design must respect Saibai community value and water usage practices, for example, separate rainwater and lagoon design respect the traditional rainwater harvesting and improve the safety of lagoon source, so it prioritizes local control for using easy control, understand and maintain for avoiding long-term dependence on specialist.

Cultural Respect

Separate rainwater and lagoon design respects traditional rainwater harvesting practices while improving lagoon source safety. This approach prioritizes local control through systems that are easy to control, understand and maintain.

Community Empowerment

Avoiding long-term dependence on specialists by designing systems that community members can operate and maintain independently.

Local Capacity Building

Training local operators creates employment opportunities, builds community pride, and ensures sustainable system maintenance.

Environmental Sustainability

Chemical-Free Disinfection

Using UV sterilization can replace chlorine to realize chemical free disinfection which do not need the imported chemicals for reduce the problem of the risk of chemical byproducts entering the environment.

Waste Management Protocol: The protocol of waste management must be planned to remove, handle and recycle the non-local waste for protecting environment of island because the waste filter cartridges and UV lamps will be produced for the large value. The material prioritized to the local for minimize imported waste.

Community Health and Economic Benefits

Key Benefits to Saibai Community:

  • Water Quality Improvement: The water quality must below 1 NTU which build the trust in water apply for enhance the confident from community to reduce energy-intensive household boiling practice.
  • Economic Savings: Eliminating the need of boiling water translates low the household energy cost which can provide the benefit to residents.
  • Local Employment: The system uses the simple and low maintenance design to avoid the complex machinery and hold the training for local operator which can local employment, build pride, community ownership and ensuring easy maintenance.
  • Community Ownership: Simple and maintainable systems foster pride and long-term community engagement.

Community Adoption Process

The implementation follows a carefully planned journey to ensure community acceptance and long-term success:

1. Awareness & Education

Community workshops and demonstrations to build understanding of the new water system benefits and operation.

2. Training & Skill Building

Hands-on training sessions for local operators to develop maintenance and troubleshooting skills.

3. System Operation

Gradual transfer of operational responsibility to trained community members with ongoing support.

4. Community Ownership

Full local management of water systems, creating sustainable employment and community pride.

Integrated Success: By addressing cultural, environmental, and community considerations together, the water system design achieves technical excellence while respecting local values, protecting the environment, and empowering the Saibai community for long-term sustainability.
